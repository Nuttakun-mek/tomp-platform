# Publish, Change, and Recovery Standard

## Publish

Publish converts a working plan into an operational baseline.

Before publish:

- Direct editing is allowed by authorized users.
- Draft versions may change.
- Drivers may not see assignments unless preview access is enabled.

After publish:

- Drivers and coordinators see assigned work.
- Organizer sees permitted scope.
- Direct edits are restricted.
- All changes must be recorded as change events.

## Change

A change is any modification after publish.

Change must include:

- Owner
- Reason
- Impact
- Affected mission
- Affected assignment
- Affected driver/vehicle/call sign
- Approval if required
- Notification scope
- Timeline event

## Change Levels

| Level | Type | Example |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Information | Note update |
| 2 | Operational | Driver replacement |
| 3 | Critical | Mission time change |
| 4 | Emergency | Session cancellation |

## Recovery

Recovery is the action taken to bring service back to commitment.

Recovery types:

- Driver replacement
- Vehicle replacement
- Route change
- Schedule change
- Manual confirmation
- Standby deployment
- Mission cancellation
- Reassignment

Recovery must never erase the original plan. It must preserve history and record outcome.
