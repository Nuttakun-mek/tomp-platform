# Product Constitution

## Purpose

TOMP exists to help event transportation teams plan, coordinate, execute, monitor, and improve operations with a reliable operating baseline and complete operational history.

## Product Identity

TOMP is a Transportation Operations Management Platform.

TOMP is not:

- Fleet management.
- ERP.
- CRM.
- A navigation app.
- A driver control system.

## Operating Thesis

Transportation operations need a shared plan, controlled publication, live coordination, and traceable adaptation. The value of TOMP comes from making the operation understandable before, during, and after execution.

## Core Workflow

1. Plan the project, missions, assignments, call signs, vehicles, drivers, and time windows.
2. Prepare access, readiness, and operational checks.
3. Operate using the published plan as the baseline.
4. Adapt through explicit change events after publish.
5. Improve using timeline, audit, exceptions, and post-operation review.

## Non-Negotiables

- Project is the operational container.
- Mission is the service activity.
- Assignment connects mission, driver, vehicle, call sign, and time window.
- Call Sign is operational identity, not vehicle plate.
- Published plan is the baseline.
- After publish, changes must be recorded as change events.
- Timeline and audit are mandatory.
- Google Maps is for navigation; TOMP is for operations management.
- GPS supports visibility but does not control drivers.
- QR access is primary for temporary drivers.
- Operation users use login.
- Coordinator and organizer access can be scoped.
- Zero-cost-first is a launch constraint, not an architectural ceiling.

## Product Values

- Operational truth over decorative dashboards.
- Clear baseline before live coordination.
- Traceability over silent mutation.
- Fast field access without weakening audit.
- Practical navigation handoff instead of rebuilding maps.
- Modular growth without premature platform sprawl.
