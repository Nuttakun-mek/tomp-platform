# Business Model

## Business Context

The company already operates transportation management services using paper, Excel, phone calls, messaging apps, meetings, and experienced operators.

TOMP is introduced to elevate existing operations, not to replace the business model.

## Business Problem

Current operations rely heavily on:

- High-skill people.
- Many phone calls.
- Many communication channels.
- Repeated data entry.
- Onsite coordination.
- Manual problem solving.
- Individual memory and experience.

As business grows, this creates operational bottlenecks.

## Business Goal

Use technology to make existing service delivery more efficient, consistent, scalable, and professional.

## Primary Business Outcomes

1. Same team can handle more projects.
2. Fewer calls are required.
3. Fewer onsite staff are required for the same quality.
4. New staff can perform closer to experienced staff.
5. Customers feel more confident.
6. Operations identify issues earlier.
7. Lessons from one project improve the next project.

## Business KPIs

- Projects per operation staff
- Average planning time
- Call count per project
- Readiness completion rate
- On-time commitment rate
- Average recovery time
- Incident count
- Customer confidence score
- Organizer follow-up count
- Driver activation completion rate
